Paintings

The Oxbow

Artist: Thomas Cole

The Oxbow, is a seminal American landscape painting by Thomas Cole, founder of the Hudson River School. The 1836 painting depicts a Romantic panorama of the Connecticut River Valley just after a thunderstorm.

Water Lilies and Japanese Bridge.

Artist: Claude Monet

This 1899 painting is beautiful in its arrangement of backdrop forests, the famous bridge, and weeping willows. It’s made up of quick strokes, which he used a lot in his later years.

Sculptues

Ashoka Pillar, Sarnath

Artist: Ashoka circa

Four lions are seated back to back. At present the Column remains in the same place whereas the Lion Capital is at the Sarnath Museum. This Lion Capital of Ashoka from Sarnath has been adopted as the National Emblem of India and the wheel ""Ashoka Chakra"" from its base was placed onto the centre of the flag of India.

Spitzhacke

Artist: Claes Oldenburg

The sculpture 'Spitzhacke' depicts a pickaxe and suggests that it’s “breaking ground” in a park in Kassel, Germany. Art enthusiasts remember Oldenburg for his outlandish creations, whimsical style, and unique approach to pop art.
